How It Works

From packed to snow-ready in under 2 minutes

Step 1: Unpack

Each Airlite Snowshoe comes in a compact drawstring bag that fits easily in a backpack, saddlebag, snowmachine storage compartment, or vehicle trunk. When the situation calls for snowshoes, simply pull them out of the bag and unfold.

Step 2: Initial Inflation by Mouth

Begin by blowing up the snowshoe using the quick inflation/deflation valve. This gets the basic shape established and provides initial firmness. Each unit has two valves — start with the larger quick-inflate valve for the initial fill.

Step 3: Firm Up with Pump or CO2

After the initial mouth inflation, use the standard tire valve (compatible with most small hand pumps or CO2 devices) to bring the snowshoe to full operating pressure. The preferred method is a quick shot of CO2 or a few strokes of a compact hand pump. This step is critical for proper performance.

Step 4: Check Pressure

Inflate until the toe area is firm — between 8.5 and 10.5 PSI. Use the included pressure gauge to verify. Proper inflation ensures the snowshoe provides adequate flotation and support in deep snow. Using the product without full inflation is not recommended.

Step 5: Strap In and Go

Secure your boots to the snowshoe using the integrated binding system. The traction pad on the bottom provides grip in most snow conditions. For icy or hard-packed surfaces, attach the optional crampons (included in the Complete Package) for additional traction.

After Use: Deflation and Storage

When you're done, open the quick deflation valve to release air rapidly. Fold the snowshoes and return them to the drawstring storage bags. The compact design means they take up minimal space and are always ready for the next time you need them.

Recommended Inflation Devices

  • CO2 Inflators: Red Zeppelin by Planet Bike — compact and powerful, our personal favorite
  • Hand Pumps: Crank Brothers Power Pump — small, lightweight, reliable
  • Any standard tire valve pump — most cycling pumps and portable air compressors work

Maintenance Tips

  • Inspect for damage before and after each use
  • Store in a cool, dry place away from direct sunlight
  • Keep the patch kit accessible in case of field repairs
  • Check valve seals periodically for proper function
  • Clean with mild soap and water if needed — avoid harsh chemicals
